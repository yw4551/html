music history

Already in the Bible we see that the father of the percussion instrument (violin and harp) was Yuval ben Lemech and in fact he was the inventor of the instrument.
There are few archeological finds of simple musical instruments from the Paleolithic period. The oldest example is a flute made on a long bone of an eagle that was discovered in a cave in Germany from the Uriniak culture about 40,000 years ago. Anthropologists estimate that music evolved in early times initially as an imitation of animal sounds. The next stage in the development of music is when it was already written and noted as "ancient music".
The oldest "song" in the world is, according to Anne Drapcorn-Kilmer, who researched the subject, written in cuneiform and originated in light about 4,000 years ago. Drapcorn-Kilmar found a triangular rhythm (found in ancient Egyptian poetry) written on a diatonic scale. There is some evidence from ancient times that suggests the use of various musical instruments - paintings on urns and written descriptions of music indicate the use of musical instruments such as double tubes that are like a primitive flute and polyphony. Descriptions of similar developments appear in both Indus Valley culture and Aristotle's writings.
In prehistoric times music was probably based on rhythm and melody and harmony came at a much later time. Prehistoric music seems to have been played by percussion (stones, sticks, bones, hunting instruments) and human voice. It is not known how ancient music really sounded and scholars are only able to guess it from finds from the Early Stone Age such as bones with holes punched in their sides. Musical instruments such as a seven-hole flute and various types of strings have also been found at archeological sites of the Indus Valley culture.

types of music

Almost all genres of popular music in the last century came from the United States. This is where African and European musical traditions came together and it is this blend of traditions that has spawned popular music. A genre of music is a conventional category that identifies parts of music as belonging to a common tradition or group of conventions. Music can be divided into different types in many different ways.
